The Fall of the Axis powers

The Axis powers, consisting primarily of Germany, Italy, and Japan, faced significant defeats during World War II.

The Fall of Germany

Germany's expansionist goals led to the invasion of multiple countries, including Poland, France, and the Soviet Union. However, the turning point came with the Allied forces' successful D-Day invasion in 1944, leading to the liberation of France and the subsequent advance into Germany.

The Fall of Italy

Italy faced internal dissent and military setbacks, leading to the fall of Mussolini's fascist regime in 1943.

The Fall of Japan

Japan's expansion in the Pacific was halted by decisive naval battles, such as Midway and Coral Sea. The dropping of atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki in August 1945 forced Japan's surrender, marking the end of World War II.
